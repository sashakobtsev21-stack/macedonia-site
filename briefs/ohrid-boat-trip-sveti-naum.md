## Facts to use

- Ohrid region is a UNESCO mixed World Heritage property. UNESCO states that the Lake Ohrid region was first inscribed for nature conservation values in 1979 and cultural heritage values in 1980, then extended into Albania in 2019.
- St. Naum Monastery is about 29 km south of Ohrid town, near the Albanian border. It was founded by St. Naum of Ohrid around 905 AD; St. Naum is buried in the church.
- The springs by St. Naum are karst springs feeding Lake Ohrid / Black Drim area. Small rowing boats operate on the spring pools.
- Bay of Bones / Museum on Water is at Plocha Michov Grad near Gradiste/Pestani. Local museum/Cultural Heritage Protection Office describes it as a reconstruction of a prehistoric pile-dwelling settlement; investigations found piles and artifacts from the Late Bronze Age / Early Iron Age, commonly presented as about 1200-700 BC. The reconstructed settlement opened as Museum on Water in 2008.
- Boat schedules/prices change by operator and season. Checked 2026-06-26: Armada Boat lists a St. Naum cruise from Ohrid port at 10:15, return from St. Naum at 15:15, arrival back around 17:00, cruise duration about 1h20 one way, adult ticket 1,200 MKD / EUR 20, child ticket 600 MKD / EUR 10. Their page says final schedules are confirmed at least 7 days in advance and online booking is May-September. Use this as an operator example, not a universal promise.
- Armada notes extras are not included: Bay of Bones museum around 150 MKD pp, St. Naum monastery around 150 MKD pp, rowing boat through Crn Drim springs about EUR 5 pp, food/drinks extra. Phrase as checked operator guidance and tell readers to confirm current inclusions.
