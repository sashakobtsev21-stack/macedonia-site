Vinoteka Temov is the place to get to grips with Macedonian wine, which is good and still largely unknown in the West. Set in a restored Old Bazaar house near the Church of Sveti Spas, it is widely credited as the country's first dedicated wine bar. The list runs deep on home-grown labels — the Tikveš region in particular — alongside cheese plates and a few hot dishes to go with the glass. Staff are happy to steer you through unfamiliar grapes like Vranec and Temjanika. Prices for wine sit at the higher end for the region, so this is a tasting destination rather than a budget meal. Pair it with a wander through the bazaar and a meal nearby.
