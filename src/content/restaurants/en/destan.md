Destan is the classic kebapi stop in Skopje's Old Bazaar and, by most accounts, the oldest restaurant in the city — grilling since 1913. The menu barely changes: kebapi (small grilled rolls of minced meat) served by the portion with kajmak, chopped raw onion, grilled peppers and warm bread, plus a šopska salad on the side. Meat is on the grill constantly, so you rarely wait long. It is cheap, busy and best enjoyed at one of the outdoor tables under the bazaar's open sky. Prices are in Macedonian denar (MKD); a full portion is one of the best-value meals in town. There are a couple of other Destan branches around the city, but the Old Bazaar original is the one to seek out.
