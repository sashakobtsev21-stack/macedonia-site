## Why Lake Ohrid

Lake Ohrid is one of Europe’s oldest and deepest lakes (about 1.2 million years old, up to 288 m deep) and a UNESCO World Heritage site of mixed type — natural and cultural. Its waters hold more than 200 endemic species, including the famous Ohrid trout.

The lakeside **old town of Ohrid** is the cultural half of the listing: Byzantine churches such as St. John at Kaneo and St. Sophia, the Plaošnik complex, and Samuel’s Fortress above the rooftops.

## Good to know

Ohrid is the country’s main tourism anchor — a large share of North Macedonia’s overnight stays happen here, peaking in July–August. The lake is shared with Albania, which makes a two-country lake route possible.
